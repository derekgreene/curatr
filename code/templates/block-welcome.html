Curatr is an online platform designed to improve the accessibility of the British Library Nineteenth Century Digitised Books Collection (BL19) for humanities scholars. The collection comprises {{ num_books }} unique English-language titles, both fiction and non-fiction, spanning {{ year_min }} to {{ year_max }}, and incorporates the first digitised version of the topical classification system used by the British Library from the early nineteenth century until the 1980s.

The platform requires no extensive technical training and offers comprehensive search functionality by keyword, author, category, and date. Its advanced keyword search and recommendation functions help locate research topics within little-known or long unwieldy texts, while semantic network visualisations surface overlooked works and identify related terminology and thematic associations across the BL19 collection. Curatr also supports the development and export of sub-corpora, defined thematically, chronologically, and by classification. Instructional videos on using Curatr are available on the VICTEUR project website.

This project has received funding from the European Research Council (ERC) under the European Union’s Horizon 2020 research and innovation programme (grant agreement No 884951) and is based at University College Dublin. The project is being undertaken by members of the UCD English, Drama and Film, in collaboration with researchers from the Insight Research Ireland Centre for Data Analytics at UCD. The BL19 digitised book collection was provided by British Library Labs.
